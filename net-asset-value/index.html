What is Net Asset Value

0

Net asset value = (Assets – Liabilities) ÷ (Outstanding shares)

Investors use net asset value formula to figure out the equity value per share. Here the equity is also known as mutual fund which is basically an investment sub-divided into shares which are then owned by investors. In the investment pool each share has a weight according to its value. The aim behind this approach is to diversify & minimize the risk.

You must know about some basic assumptions or limitations which are inherit in net asset value approach. It do not take in to account the future dividends and growth unlike other valuation approaches for stock and bonds. This formula only shows the per share value based on the net assets of a company.

To determine the net asset value the price of shares is used to evaluate the company’s mutual fund worth. Due to the reason that stocks are sold directly by company instead of exchange or secondary market stock and bond valuation methods are not used. On the other side, in reality stocks are sold by using biding and secondary market pricing where investor look in to the potential of future expected earnings and factors also.

How net asset value formula is useful for investors

As we say earlier, that to calculate net asset value formula we use the mutual funds of company to evaluate the price of share. However, the net asset value formula may not be useful enough for an investor especially for investment decision making purpose. On the other side one must remember that this formula do not take in to account the expected future earnings of shares while calculations.

Net asset value formula illustration

Lets assume that Alpha LLC have a mutual funds with assets of $1.5 million, and 87,000 outstanding shares with $130,000 liabilities. Now with this information we try to calculate the net asset value for Alpha LLC.

Value per share = ($1,500,000 – $130,000) ÷ $87,000
= $15.75

So, the value per share is $15.75
