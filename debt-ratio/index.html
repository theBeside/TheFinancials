What is Debt Ratio

0

Debt Ratio = (Total liablities)/(Total Assets)

Debt ratio helps an investor OR financial analyst to determine the liquidity situation of a company. This ratio tells about the ability of a company to pay-back its liabilities by using its available assets. In other words a person is critically looking in to a situation if liquidity occurs.

One more thing to remember here is that do not mix up debt ratio with debt to income ratio as it measures personal capacity while debt ratio measures capacity of a company. Debt to income ratio is most commonly used by lending companies to determine the pay-back capacity of a client.

How debt ratio is useful for investors

Typically, debt ratio is used along with many other ratios to analyze the financial leverage of a company and to measure the ability for meeting its obligations. If the calculations show significant figures then it is a clear indication that debt is too much high and in foreseeable future company may find it difficult to maintain its operations or get new debt. Just like any other case where banks or other lending institutions evaluate the risk involved for non-payment while issuing a new loan to its customer.

For calculations purpose, the values which one must have for calculation purpose can be found from balance sheet of a company.

Debt ratio formula examples

Let’s suppose Wood & Spines is a company which manufactures wooden doors for its clients. An investor is looking in to invest in the company and wants to calculate debt ratio before making any decision. He provides the information about values required for calculation purpose includes total assets of $2.8 million and total liabilities of $1.2 million. By putting values in the formula we get;

Debt Ratio = 2,800,000 ÷ 1,200,000

= 2.333

Internal vs. external use of debt ratio

The debt ratio can be used by a company for internal use to check out its current position in liquidity or financial leverage terms. Although debt ratio is most commonly used by investors and debtors to evaluate the risk of investment but, internal use has also been seen. As with other financial ratios the same case is with debt ratio where one cannot use this formula alone to evaluate all aspects of a company. To enjoy a perfect business with a company one must evaluate the deep details rather than a macro-level view.
